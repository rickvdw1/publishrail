# Brand voice — TaskFlow (example)

> Copy this file to `context/messaging.md` and replace with your own brand voice guidelines.

## Tone

**Dry and direct.** We write like an engineering lead explaining a system to a colleague — not like a marketing team pitching to a prospect.

- No superlatives ("best", "most powerful", "leading")
- No urgency ("don't miss out", "today only")
- No vague benefit claims ("improve your productivity", "boost collaboration")
- Yes to specific outcomes: "sprint planning takes 30 minutes instead of 2 hours"
- Yes to honest trade-offs: "TaskFlow works best for teams that already have defined sprint rhythms"

## Voice

**Confident but not arrogant.** We acknowledge where competitors are genuinely strong. We name the gap clearly without mocking them.

**Concrete over abstract.** Instead of "improves coordination", say "when a dependency slips, the affected sprint items are flagged automatically before standup."

**Short sentences.** Our readers are scanning, not studying.

## Article tone

- Write to an engineering manager who has seen 10 project management tools and is tired of vendor marketing.
- Assume the reader knows what a sprint is, what a backlog is, what a roadmap is.
- Don't explain basic concepts. Go straight to the differentiator.
- End comparison articles with a clear verdict, not a "it depends" hedge.

## Things we never say

- "Transform your workflow"
- "Leverage our platform"
- "Seamlessly integrate"
- "Actionable insights"
- "Best-in-class"
- "Delve into"
- "It's worth noting that"
