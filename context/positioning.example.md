# Positioning guide — TaskFlow (example)

> Copy this file to `context/positioning.md` and replace with your own company's positioning.

## What TaskFlow is

TaskFlow is a project management platform built for engineering and product teams that need more than simple task lists. It connects work across sprints, backlogs, and roadmaps — giving every contributor clarity on what to build next and why.

TaskFlow is not a communication tool, a document editor, or a BI dashboard. It is a coordination system: it tracks work, surfaces dependencies, and connects individual tasks to project goals automatically.

## The problem we solve

Most teams manage work in tools that were designed for individual productivity, not team coordination. Work gets duplicated across Jira, Notion, Linear, spreadsheets, and Slack. Dependencies are invisible. Priorities are unclear. Sprint planning is a meeting instead of a system.

TaskFlow replaces that fragmentation with a single coordination layer that all contributors use without extra context-switching.

## Core differentiators

1. **Unified backlog with auto-priority** — TaskFlow scores backlog items continuously based on dependency chains, team capacity, and goal alignment. No manual sorting.

2. **Dependency tracking across teams** — Cross-team dependencies are surfaced automatically. Blocked work is flagged before it blocks a sprint.

3. **Goal-connected tasks** — Every task is traceable to a project goal. Progress is visible without status meetings.

4. **No workflow change required** — Engineers continue using their preferred coding tools. TaskFlow integrates with GitHub, GitLab, Jira, and Linear — it does not replace them.

## What TaskFlow is not

- Not a chat tool (use Slack for that)
- Not a document editor (use Notion for that)
- Not a BI tool (use Looker for that)
- Does not replace engineering judgment about what to build

## Vocabulary

**Use:** coordination, dependency, goal-connected, auto-prioritized, backlog clarity, capacity, sprint coordination, unified view

**Avoid:** "platform" (too generic), "all-in-one solution", "transform your workflow", "seamless", "leverage", "actionable insights"

## Audience

Primary: Engineering managers, product managers, heads of engineering at teams of 10–200 engineers
Secondary: CTOs and VPs of Product at scaling startups
Not: Individual freelancers, large enterprises with procurement requirements
