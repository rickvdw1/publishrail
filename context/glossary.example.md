## Core terms

**Backlog** — The full list of planned work items that have not yet been assigned to a sprint or milestone.

**Sprint** — A fixed time-box (usually 1–2 weeks) in which a team commits to completing a defined set of work items.

**Dependency** — A relationship between two work items where one cannot start or complete until the other does.

**Goal** — A project-level outcome that a set of work items contributes to. Goals in TaskFlow are set at the project level and are visible on every connected task.

**Capacity** — The total available engineering hours in a sprint, calculated from team size and planned time off.

**Auto-priority score** — TaskFlow's internal score for each backlog item, calculated from dependency urgency, goal alignment, and capacity fit. Updated continuously.

**Sprint coordination** — The process of ensuring all sprint items are properly scoped, assigned, and unblocked before the sprint starts.

## What NOT to call things

| Avoid | Use instead |
|---|---|
| "Project management platform" | "coordination system" or "project coordination tool" |
| "All-in-one tool" | (just describe what it does) |
| "AI-powered" | only say AI when describing the auto-priority feature specifically |
| "Workflow automation" | "dependency tracking" or "auto-priority" |

When writing comparison articles, do not use competitors' branded vocabulary (e.g. "Jira Epics", "Linear Cycles", "Notion Databases") to describe TaskFlow's features. Use TaskFlow's own terminology.
